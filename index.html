Frequently Asked Questions

Who can come?

All current undergraduate and graduate students at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville are guaranteed spots. Current undergraduate and undergraduate students at other universities are encouraged to register and we will try to accomodate as many attendees as possible.

Will there be free food, drinks, and caffiene?

Yes! Absolutely! We will provide catered meals, drinks at all times, snack breaks, and endless caffiene.

Where do I sleep?

We will have a designated quiet sleeping area on site, and you are encouraged to bring sleeping bags, pillows, toiletries, and anything else you need to sleep. There are not showers at the venue, so please be prepared to go without showering unless you have other accomodations.

How much does it cost?

The event is completely free for eligible students (see first question above). Absolutely nothing! The event is completely free for eligible and registered students. We will provide you with the venue, food, Wi-Fi, etc. You just need to show up.

Do I need to stay the entire time?

Nope, we are not a lock-in. We realize that 36 hours is a long time, and that some people might have other commitments during that time. Feel free to stay and leave as you choose. However, we would prefer that people stay to promote the environment.

Where do I park?

If you're on campus already and have a parking permit, park where you would normally park. For attendees coming from other universities, we will distribute information to registred attendees prior to the event regarding where to park.

I have never been to a hackathon before, what should I do?

No worries at all. We will be providing mentors during the hackathon and running workshops, as well as providing food and drinks and a place to sleep if you don't have one. We will also have a Slack team for anyone to get help at during the hackathon.

When and where is VolHacks happening?

VolHacks will take place on September 30th to October 2nd, at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville in the foothills of the Great Smoky Mountains.

But I don't have an idea, what should I do?

Don't worry at all! We will walk you through how to brainstorm great ideas at the beginning of VolHacks.

How do teams work?

There is a maximum of 4 people per team. Once the idea brainstorming session is done, there will be an open mic and networking time to find a team. Everyone registered will also be invited to the VolHacks Slack team prior to the hackathon in order to network and discuss ideas.

What should I bring?

You will definitely need your laptop, phone, their chargers, as well as a valid student or government ID. Feel free to also bring along a sleeping bag, and we recommend some comfortable clothes.

Is there a Code of Conduct?

Yes, we abide by the Major League Hacking Code of Conduct.
